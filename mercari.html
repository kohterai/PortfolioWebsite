Design Critique of メルカリ

by Koh Terai

Preface

I'm Koh Terai and I recent graduate of NYU Abu Dhabi and currently a freelance designer based in Tokyo.

This design critique is based on Mercari (v3.33.0) running on iOS version 10.3.3 on an iPhone 5 (ME043J/A). These are my initial observations based on simple testing of the Mercari app. Below are my thoughts on what can be improved to bring the experience of the app to the next level. These would be the first things I would get fixed if I joined the Mercari design team.

Terminology

For clarity throughout the critique the following are the terms that will be used to identify the different components inside the app.

Interactions

Lagging Section Selector

The swiping between different categories on the main screen currently feels slow and unresponsive. The reason for this is because the section selector does not respond to movements of the feed section.

This behavior becomes a problem when a user swipes from one feed to another, since the section selector only responds after the feed is finished moving to a new section.

fig. 1 - Movement of the section selector drags behind the feed section. There is a 180~190ms delay between the completion of all the animations and the red section indicator text to change.

This lack of immediate responsiveness of the section selector makes the app feel slow and low-tech. In fact, there are strange delays between the animations.

Possible fix

Creating some sort of mapping between the movement of the feed section and the section selector is ideal. This behavior can be seen in the Facebook messenger app (v142.0). The selector bar moves (blue bar on messenger), but the text remains highlighted to indicate the current position of the feed for when the user releases their touch midway of a swipe.

fig. 2 - No mapping between movement of feed section and category selector.
fig. 3 - Facebook messenger has a mapping between movement of feed section and category selector (blue bar).

This design pattern may be harder to implement for Mercari’s section selector since the menu option would need to move back to the center once the feed moves to the next section.

A simple solution would be to create a direct mapping between the movement of the section selector and feed section (but with the red bar static at the central position).

fig. 4 - Current motion on category selector on Mercari.
fig. 5 - Simulation of motion with a mapping between category selector and feed section.

Unresponsive Category Selector

Skimming through the category selector lacks feedback that the skimming causes any effects. It feels dead.

Possible fix

The red indicator bar changes lengths as it locks into a new section.

fig. 6 - Current motion on category selector on Mercari (unresponsive).
fig. 7 - Simulation of motion with a mapping between category selector and feed section.

Jumping Header

Another problem is with the transition of the header hiding.

When the header hides, it seems like the header jumps up and causes a jittery transition. In fact, the cause of this is that the hiding of the iOS status bar is not animated and simply disappears. This causes the screen to momentarily jitter and make it seem like the app is slowing down the phone. This problem is quite evident in slow motion.

fig. 8 - iOS status bar suddenly dissapears causing the header to jump up disrupting a smooth transition.
fig. 9 - iOS status bar dissapearing (in slow motion).

In fact the hiding of the iOS status bar causes additional problems. The section selector is so thin that the pulldown menu for the spotlight search gets triggered. In order to browse through the section selector, users must dodge the spotlight tab.

fig. 10 - iOS spotlight search pulldown tab gets in the way of the section selector.

Possible fix

Don’t hide the status bar. In fact the status bar does not hide anywhere else in the app so it doesn't make sense to just hide it there.

User Flow

Can't get back to home screen

There is one huge problem that when a user goes deeper and deeper, it becomes impossible to quickly go back to the home screen. Users need to repeatedly click on the back button.

fig. 11 - Once users have spent a lot of time browsing and dug deep into the app, users must repeatedly click the back button in order to get back to the home screen. There is no magical button to quickly take users back to the main screen.

Possible fix

Other commerce mobile apps deal with this by either leaving the header accesible throughout the app or having tab bars at the bottom. It is actually quicker to close the app once, and restart to quickly return back to the home screen.

fig. 12 - The Amazon app has the Amazon logo accessible at the top of the screen throughout the app that users can click on to immediately goto the top screen.
fig. 13 - The eBay app has a tab bar at the bottom that users can click on to immediately return to the home screen.

End Note

I have many more thoughts about the search functionality, filtering, also inconsistencies between the Global Mercari apps and the Japanese version. I hope this design critique serves as a basic demonstration of my capabilities and attention to detail in building a product.

As one of Japan's largest startups, I believe that if Mercari embraces design as one of the core values of the product (like AirBnb, Stripe, Apple) we can set an example for other Japanese tech companies how important great design is.
